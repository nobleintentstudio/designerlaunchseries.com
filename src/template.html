Designer Launch
Series

Getting your career started is hard.
This 4-part series will make it easier.

Starting your career in any field today is hard enough, especially for creative professionals—and we've decided to do something about it.

If you're currently enrolled in school or just beginning your career, we'd like to invite you to join our Designer Launch Series. Together, we'll help you connect what you know with what you need and make sure you're set up for success in the process.

Event Schedule

Stage 1: Design thinking

September 26, 2019
3:45pm - 6:00pm

First things first—a deep dive into Design Thinking! You'll explore how it relates to the design process and witness first hand how a designer can use it to produce stronger design results. You'll focus on connecting the dots between theory and practice, then extending design thinking into other applications outside of design. A brief presentation (15 – 20 minutes) is included, along with a hands-on exercise to bring design thinking to life.

Stage 2: Customer empathy

November 14, 2019
3:45pm - 6:00pm

Understanding actual customers is a critical component in delivering powerful, relevant work. Customer empathy via a Design for Delight (D4D) workshop, provides you with a dynamic, hands-on, small group activity to understand rapid prototyping (led by Intuit’s Justin Marr) centered around a customer need (using a real client). This will be followed up by a panel discussion of what it feels like to work with a customer – the reality of expectations, an honest look at mistakes made and the importance of doing your research.

Stage 3: Bridging the gap

February 20, 2020
3:45pm - 6:00pm

We'll be talking about Bridging the Gap—filling that considerable void between school and the design business. You'll meet a panel of designers who now "do the hiring" but once walked in the same shoes you're wearing. They'll share their thoughts on best path forward, what they might have done a little differently and the benefits of failing fast, wrapping up with plenty of time for Q&A.

Stage 4: Understanding the disciplines

May 7, 2020
3:45pm - 6:00pm

Lastly, you'll have the opportunity to put into practice all of your learned disciplines during a 60 minute design challenge, presenting your final solution to a panel of AIGA team members. We'll celebrate your efforts and endurance with a graduation ceremony, certificate and newly bonded friendships.

FAQs

Who is Intuit?

Intuit is the software company that makes Mint, TurboTax, Turbo, and QuickBooks. They have been named to Fast Company’s top 10 companies for design. More than 100 designers currently work in the company’s San Diego Design Studio.

Will there be time to network with designers at each session?

Yes! There will be time before and after each session to meet working designers and ask your burning questions about how they got their careers started.

Do I have to pay for parking?

No, parking is free. There is a parking structure adjacent to the building that houses the Intuit Design Studio.

What is the closest bus stop?

Unfortunately, the Metropolitan Transit System runs only a few bus routes in the area. The closest stop is on Route 20 in Rancho Penasquitos, about 3 miles away from the Intuit Design Studio.

Will the sessions start promptly at 4?

Registration and check-in will take place from 3:45 – 4:00pm, and refreshments will be available. Each session will begin promptly at 4:00pm.

Do I have to go to all four Designer Launch Series sessions?

No, you may go to one, some or all, but you’ll learn the most and make the most contacts if you attend all four.

If I register for all four but can’t attend one of them, can I get a refund?

For any refunds or registration-related questions, contact info@sandiego.aiga.org

How will the Uber promo code work?

For early registrants who have limited transportation options, we can offer an Uber code that will be good only for rides to and from the Intuit Design Studio, and only during the hours of 3 and 8 p.m. the day of each session. If you need an Uber promo code, contact info@sandiego.aiga.org

How much does it cost to attend?

Free to AIGA Student Members and Early Career Designers (defined as 1 – 3 years of work experience)

$10 per Stage or $30 for all 4 stages for non-member students

$25 per Stage or $75 for all 4 Stages for non-member early career designers
